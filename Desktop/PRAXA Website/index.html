Healthcare & care · Use case 1

Breaking difficult news to a family member

Maria's care center uploads its internal communication guidelines, the palliative transition conversation framework, and the relevant sections of its quality management handbook into PRAXA Skills.

Maria then practices in three 15-minute sessions — with the AI avatar playing the worried, emotionally overwhelmed daughter.

Round 1 — Finding the right words

Maria stops herself mid-conversation when she realizes her phrasing is more direct than the guidelines recommend. Feedback shows her alternatives based on the uploaded house standard.

Round 2 — The transition

She practices moving from the status update to the palliative care recommendation — the hardest structural moment in the conversation.

Round 3 — Emotional escalation

The avatar accuses, cries, and places blame. Maria practices de-escalation under real emotional pressure.

OutcomeMaria enters the real conversation feeling like she's already been through it. Her language is precise, her presence is calm.

Why this works

Trained on your organization's actual guidelines — not generic content

PRAXA Skills doesn't simulate a generic palliative care conversation. It simulates your version — with your care home's exact framework, your documentation requirements, and your quality standards embedded in every response and piece of feedback.

The gap between knowing the guidelines and performing them under emotional pressure is exactly what no seminar can close. PRAXA Skills closes it.

Vocational training · Use case 2

The first customer phone call in a trades business

The business accesses PRAXA Skills through a license arranged by their regional training provider. The trainer spends 20 minutes uploading the essentials: the greeting formula, the five most common call types, the fault-report checklist, and three typical complaint scenarios.

Lukas practices independently — on the company tablet in the workshop before his shift starts.

Scenario 1 — "Frau Huber" reports a broken heating

Lukas has to ask the right questions, capture the report in a structured way, and communicate the next step clearly.

Scenario 2 — "Herr Brandner" is angry

The technician didn't show up yesterday. Lukas practices staying factual, apologizing without making promises he can't keep, and routing the issue correctly.

OutcomeThe trainer saves individual coaching time without compromising quality. The tool becomes a permanent part of the training program — ready for the next apprentice in September.

Why this works

The trainer sets it up once. The apprentice practices as often as needed.

In small and medium-sized businesses, structured communication training rarely exists — not because it isn't needed, but because no one has the time to deliver it consistently. PRAXA Skills solves this without requiring the trainer to be present.

The apprentice can run the same scenario a dozen times before the first real call. That repetition — safely, without judgment — builds confidence.

Leadership development · Use case 3

A formal performance conversation after repeated underdelivery

The company has introduced PRAXA Skills as part of its leadership development program. Uploaded: the internal conversation structure for documented performance discussions, the HR policy on legally relevant documentation, the code of conduct, and the works agreement on performance assessment.

Round 1 — The deflector

The avatar acknowledges the problem but relativizes every concrete example. Sandra practices staying with facts and not getting drawn into justification debates.

Round 2 — The confrontational one

The avatar threatens to escalate to the works council. She practices holding the frame while not destroying the relationship.

Round 3 — The emotional one

The avatar breaks down and hints at personal problems. Sandra practices transitioning from criticism to support without losing the core message.

OutcomeSandra leads the conversation prepared for moments no seminar could have anticipated. HR receives a legally sound record. The employee feels heard — and the team can move forward.

Why this works

The gap between a written guideline and a real person sitting across from you.

HR can send the framework. But between "Step 3: state expectations clearly" and the reality of someone who deflects, escalates, or breaks down — there's a gap no document closes. PRAXA Skills closes it.

After each round, Sandra receives structured feedback: Was the expectation stated clearly? Was a concrete agreement made? Was the documentable part closed properly?

How it works

Three steps. Real improvement.

PRAXA Skills puts learners in realistic conversations with an AI partner — then gives them specific feedback on what to do differently next time.

01

Upload your organization's context

Feed PRAXA Skills your own guidelines, playbooks, compliance standards, or conversation frameworks. Takes 20–30 minutes. The AI uses them to shape every scenario and every piece of feedback — so practice reflects your reality, not a generic template.

02

Choose a scenario and practice

Pick from 200+ pre-built workplace conversations or build your own. Voice or text. The AI plays the other party — customer, employee, candidate, family member — and responds the way a real person would, including difficult moments: deflection, emotion, escalation.

03

Get specific, structured feedback

After every session, see exactly what worked, what to adjust, and what to try next time. Not a generic score — concrete observations tied to the actual conversation and your uploaded guidelines.

04

Track progress across the team

Managers and L&D leads see usage, completion rates, and skill gaps across the whole team. Identify where to invest next. Show ROI without manual reporting.
